Title: Legally Blonde

Year: 2001
Genre: American comedy film

Director: Robert Luketic
Writers: Karen McCullah Lutz and Kirsten Smith, based on Amanda Brown's novel.

Starring: Reese Witherspoon, Luke Wilson, Selma Blair, Matthew Davis, Victor Garber, and Jennifer Coolidge.

Synopsis: The story follows Elle Woods (Reese Witherspoon), a sorority girl who attempts to win back her ex-boyfriend by pursuing a Juris Doctor degree at Harvard Law School. In the process, she overcomes stereotypes against blondes and triumphs as a successful lawyer.

Box Office Success: The film was released on July 13, 2001, grossing $142 million worldwide on an $18 million budget.

Accolades: It received positive reviews, with particular praise for Witherspoon's performance. It was nominated for a Golden Globe Award for Best Motion Picture: Musical or Comedy. Witherspoon received a Golden Globe nomination for Best Actress and won the 2002 MTV Movie Award for Best Female Performance.

Franchise: Legally Blonde has spawned a media franchise, including the sequel Legally Blonde 2: Red, White & Blonde (2003), a stage musical (2007), a direct-to-video spin-off Legally Blondes (2009), and an upcoming prequel television series, Elle (scheduled for 2026 on Amazon Prime Video).
